## Section 01: Key Bills & Legislation (past 7 days)

### Appropriation Bill, 2026 — PASSED (March 18)
Both Houses passed the Appropriation Bill, 2026, authorising ₹53.5 lakh crore withdrawal from the Consolidated Fund of India for FY 2025-26 via the guillotine procedure. FM Nirmala Sitharaman asserted the government's budgeting is "transparent and realistic," citing capex surge to ₹12.20 lakh crore (4.2x from ₹2.63 lakh crore in 2017-18).

### Finance Bill, 2026 — INTRODUCED (March 23)
FM Sitharaman tabled the Finance Bill 2026 in Lok Sabha. Key provisions include: tax-free income up to ₹12 lakh under the new regime, MAT rate reduced to 14% from 15%, TDS/TCS rationalisations, customs tariff simplification, and advance ruling validity extended from 3 to 5 years.

### Corporate Laws (Amendment) Bill, 2026 — SENT TO JPC
Introduced by FM Sitharaman in Lok Sabha, this bill amends the Limited Liability Partnership Act 2008 and Companies Act 2013 to promote startups and small businesses. Lok Sabha adopted a motion to refer it to a Joint Parliamentary Committee. Congress MP Manish Tewari opposed, citing unconstitutional delegation to subordinate legislation.

### Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Amendment Bill, 2026 — UNDER DEBATE (Introduced March 13)
The bill revises the definition of transgender persons, requires a medical board certification via the District Magistrate, and explicitly excludes persons with different sexual orientations. Opposition MPs and third-gender rights activists have demanded the government withdraw the bill, saying it erodes self-identification rights.

## Section 02: Debates & Issues (past 7 days)

### PM Modi Addresses Rajya Sabha on West Asia Crisis (March 24)
PM Modi made a detailed statement to the Rajya Sabha on the ongoing West Asia conflict. Key points: over 3,75,000 Indians safely evacuated including 1,000+ from Iran; war has created a "serious energy crisis" disrupting global trade routes; India has expanded energy import sources from 27 to 41 countries; strategic petroleum reserves at 53 lakh MT with expansion to 65 lakh MT underway. CPI(M)'s John Brittas criticised the format, saying opposition was not allowed to seek clarifications.

### Opposition Moves to Remove CEC Gyanesh Kumar
INDIA bloc filed removal notices in both Houses — 130 Lok Sabha MPs and 63 Rajya Sabha MPs — seeking removal of Chief Election Commissioner Gyanesh Kumar. The opposition has cited procedural and constitutional concerns.

### LPG Cylinder Shortage & Price Hike Debate
Congress MP Vijay Vasanth moved an adjournment motion in Lok Sabha over reported LPG cylinder shortages and underweight cylinders sold at higher prices. Oil companies have prioritised LPG for households but cut supplies to commercial users (hotels, restaurants) due to Strait of Hormuz closure.

### All-Party Meeting on Iran War (March 25)
The Centre called an all-party meeting for 5 PM on March 25 within the Parliament complex, amid opposition criticism of the government's inconsistent response to the West Asia war's economic fallout.
