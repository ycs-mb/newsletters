# US–Iran Conflict Briefing — March 22, 2026

## Escalation Level: 4/5

## Section 01: Breaking Developments (Mar 21–22)

### Trump Threatens to "Hit and Obliterate" Iranian Power Plants (Mar 22)
Trump threatened to strike Iranian power plants if Tehran does not reopen the Strait of Hormuz within 48 hours. The ultimatum marks a significant escalation in rhetoric.

### Iran Allows Japanese Ships Through Strait of Hormuz (Mar 21)
Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi announced Iran is prepared to allow Japanese-related vessels to pass through the Strait of Hormuz after consultations between officials, expanding Iran's selective blockade policy.

### Trump Considers "Winding Down" War (Mar 21)
Trump said the administration is considering "winding down" military efforts, but simultaneously deployed 2,500 additional Marines to the region and asked Congress for more war funding. Ruled out a ceasefire with Iran.

### Iranian Drones Hit Kuwait's Mina al-Ahmadi Refinery (Mar 21)
Two waves of Iranian drones struck Kuwait's Mina al-Ahmadi refinery, sparking a fire at one of the Middle East's largest facilities (730,000 bpd capacity).

### Saudi Arabia Intercepts 20 Drones (Mar 21)
Saudi defense forces downed 20 Iranian drones over eastern provinces, home to major oil installations. Bahrain reports destroying 143 missiles and 242 drones since Feb 28.

### War Powers Vote Fails in Senate (Earlier)
The Senate failed to pass a war powers resolution, allowing Trump to continue strikes without additional Congressional authorization.

### US Temporarily Lifts Iran Oil Sanctions (Mar 20)
Treasury Secretary Bessent announced temporary lifting of sanctions on Iranian oil already loaded on ships, expected to add ~140 million barrels to global supply. Valid until April 19, 2026.

## Section 02: Analysis & Strategic Highlights

### Military: Iran's "Escalate, Don't Calibrate" Strategy (CSIS)
CSIS analysis shows Iran has pursued unbridled escalation rather than calibrated retaliation, launching attacks across a dozen countries and rendering the Middle East a "no-go zone." This horizontal escalation spanning hundreds of miles is unprecedented.

### Diplomatic: UN Security Council Divided on Iran Sanctions
Russia and China challenged the legality of snapback sanctions at a March 12 Security Council meeting. The Council voted 11-2 (with China/Russia against) to proceed with debates on Iran's nuclear program.

### Humanitarian: 1,444 Killed in Iran Including 204 Children
The war has caused devastating civilian casualties, with Amnesty International highlighting the strike on a Minab elementary school that killed 170+ people, mostly schoolgirls. Over 6,668 civilian units targeted.

### International: Oil Markets in Turmoil
Brent crude surged from $73 to $120+ per barrel since the conflict began. The US war effort costs ~$900M/day. Japan has released strategic oil reserves. Oil eased to $108.84 after Trump's "winding down" comments.

### Nuclear: IAEA Reports Damage to Natanz
IAEA confirmed damage to entrance buildings at the Natanz enrichment facility. Iran has 440.9 kg of 60%-enriched uranium stored at Isfahan. IAEA has no access to any of Iran's four declared enrichment facilities.

## Section 05: Context Briefing

The US-Israeli strikes on Iran began February 28, 2026, killing Supreme Leader Khamenei and dozens of senior officials. Iran retaliated with missiles and drones across 12+ countries, effectively closing the Strait of Hormuz. Three weeks in, the conflict has no clear endgame: Trump sends mixed signals about "winding down" while deploying more troops and threatening power plants. The selective Hormuz blockade — allowing Chinese, Indian, Pakistani, and now Japanese vessels while blocking US-allied shipping — is fracturing the international response. Key escalation triggers to watch: (1) Trump's 48-hour ultimatum on power plants, (2) whether Hormuz opens further, (3) Iran's nuclear posture given IAEA access denial, (4) proxy attacks on Gulf oil infrastructure.
